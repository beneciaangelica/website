OMK Cell Suspension Study

❌ Status: Discontinued - but not a dead end!

Before I started diving deep into my current project, I explored a smaller side study focused on OMK cells, that’s short for owl monkey kidney cells. The goal was to understand how well these cells grow under different lab conditions, specifically comparing suspension culture to the more traditional monolayer format.

It was an exciting challenge at first. I tried different seeding densities and looked for signs of healthy growth. But pretty quickly, I noticed that things weren’t going as smoothly as I hoped. The cells weren’t thriving the way I expected, and I began to realize there were bigger technical hurdles to work through.

What I Tried to Explore

I was curious about whether suspension conditions might offer more flexibility for downstream experiments. I looked into how the OMK cells attached, how they responded to passaging, and what might be interfering with their ability to grow in the medium. Each attempt came with new notes, questions, and a few more variables to think about.

What I Learned and Why We Paused

Eventually, I made the decision to pause the project. At the time, I was also leading another study that was gaining momentum, and it became clear I couldn’t give both the time they deserved. Talking with my mentor, we agreed to let this one go for now.

But even though it didn’t lead to a final dataset or experiment, this experience helped me sharpen how I ask questions in the lab. It pushed me to think critically about experimental design, cell viability, and when to double down or step back. In a way, this little project taught me just as much as the bigger ones.
